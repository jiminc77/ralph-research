\begin{abstract}
Hybrid contact actions $a=(p,u)$ couple a decision about \emph{where} to act
(contact location $p$) with a decision about \emph{how} to move (motion
parameter $u$). Methods that jointly select both factors do not reveal which
factor carries the achieved one-step progress. We report a preregistered
factorial intervention study on quasi-static deformable linear object (DLO)
manipulation in a GPU-parallel rod simulator: for each canonicalized state we
execute the complete $8{\times}8$ table of contact arc-length positions
$\times$ fixed-magnitude planar motion directions and decompose the resulting
one-step progress into contact, direction, and interaction contributions. We
deliberately scope the claim narrowly: the comparison is \emph{contact-location
choice versus fixed-magnitude planar-direction choice}, not a universal
``where versus how,'' because the two factors have asymmetric expressiveness.
On a pilot of \nstatestotal{} states across three tasks
(\ncellstotal{} executed action cells, candidate \selcand{};
preregistered scale was not reachable in the available budget), the primary
contrast $\Delta_{WH}=\mathbb{E}_s[v_P-v_U]$ is
$\dwhA$~[\dwhAlo, \dwhAhi] for straightening,
$\dwhB$~[\dwhBlo, \dwhBhi] for single-bend shaping, and
$\dwhC$~[\dwhClo, \dwhChi] for endpoint repositioning
(simultaneous 95\% intervals; minimum preregistered effect $0.02$).
\resultsummary{} \absguards{} We release estimand definitions, guard thresholds, and
per-cell raw tables; we label every number as pilot-scale evidence, not
confirmatory findings.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Non-prehensile and hybrid-action manipulation methods, e.g.\ HACMan-style
actor--critic maps~\citep{zhou2023hacman} and dense affordance models for
deformable objects~\citep{wu2023foresightful}, select a contact location and a
motion parameter jointly. A successful action therefore does not identify
whether performance came from selecting \emph{where} to act, \emph{how} to
move, or a specific contact--motion pairing. This attribution gap matters for
system design: it determines whether future effort should buy dense contact
maps, richer motion generation, or explicit joint reasoning.

We study this gap with a factorial intervention on one-step, quasi-static DLO
manipulation. For a canonicalized rope state $s$ with goal $g$, we execute
\emph{every} cell of an $8{\times}8$ grid of contact positions $\times$ planar
motion directions and measure normalized one-step progress. Marginal and joint
maxima of the resulting table decompose achievable progress into a
contact-selection contribution, a direction-selection contribution, and an
interaction term that is available only under joint selection.

\paragraph{Narrow claim boundary.}
The two factors are \emph{not} symmetric in expressiveness: the contact factor
covers the full normalized arc length of the rope at 8 points, while the
motion factor covers only 8 directions in the horizontal plane at a fixed
displacement magnitude, with no magnitude, vertical, or rotational component.
Equal $8{\times}8$ cardinality equalizes search budget, not representational
completeness. All results below therefore compare \emph{contact-location
choice versus fixed-magnitude planar-direction choice}; they are not evidence
about ``where versus how'' in general, and an observed dominance of either
factor can partially reflect this parameterization asymmetry.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
(i)~A preregistered factorial design for one-step DLO action attribution with
explicit validity guards, a complete-table-or-invalid policy that separates
invalid runs from valid null results, and fixed-priority candidate selection
that never uses effect sign, magnitude, or $p$-values.
(ii)~A pilot execution of this design on three tasks in a GPU rod simulator,
with complete raw tables, controls, and uncertainty from stratified cluster
bootstrap with simultaneous intervals.
(iii)~An honest account of what a deadline-bounded budget allowed: the pilot
covers \nstatespertask{} states per task on the development split; hidden-split
confirmation and clean reproduction were not run, so no confirmatory claims
are made.

\section{Study design}
\subsection{Environment, states, and primitive}
We use a GPU-parallel discrete-rod simulator (a DLO benchmark environment
built on a differentiable rod solver~\citep{cao2026dlolab}) with a 32-vertex
rope. States are \emph{canonicalized quasi-static} configurations: an analytic
initial centerline (stratified over shape templates) is placed, velocities are
zeroed, and the rope is settled to a velocity threshold of $10^{-3}$ with an
explicit cap of $10^4$ steps. The action primitive is grasp--move--release--settle
at a chosen vertex; grasp realism is disabled in the primary condition so that
attachment is deterministic. Placement error after canonicalization is guarded
at $\le 10^{-4}$\,m per table (Section~2.4).

Tasks are t1a \emph{straighten}, t1b \emph{single bend}, and t1c
\emph{endpoint reposition}, each with stratified initial shapes
(\{u\_bend, s\_curve, random\_smooth\} for t1a; additionally \{straight\} for
t1b/t1c). Task error $D$ is a correspondence-$L_2$ distance to a goal template
(Chamfer is forbidden as primary by preregistration). A state is eligible only
if $D_{\text{before}} \ge 0.10$ (twice the success threshold), checked before
any action. Action quality is normalized one-step progress
\begin{equation}
Y(p,u) = \frac{D_{\text{before}} - D_{\text{after}}(p,u)}
              {\max(D_{\text{before}}, 10^{-8})}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Factorial action grid and candidates}
Contacts are 8 normalized arc-length positions $\sigma_i = i/7$ mapped to the
nearest native vertex; motions are 8 planar directions at $45^\circ$
increments. Candidate execution regimes fix displacement magnitude and lift
height: C0 $(0.10\,\text{m}, \text{low})$, C1 $(0.05\,\text{m}, \text{low})$,
C2 $(0.10\,\text{m}, \text{high})$, C3 $(0.05\,\text{m}, \text{high})$.
Candidate selection is \emph{fixed-priority after hard validity}
(C0$\to$C1$\to$C2$\to$C3): a candidate is selected iff it is the first whose
run passes hard validity guards; effect size, sign, and $p$-values are never
consulted. In this pilot, \selcand{} was selected\selcandreason{}.

\subsection{Estimands}
For each state's complete $8{\times}8$ table:
$v_0=\operatorname{mean}_{p,u} Y$;\;
$v_P=\max_p \operatorname{mean}_u Y$;\;
$v_U=\max_u \operatorname{mean}_p Y$;\;
$v_{PU}=\max_{p,u} Y$.
The primary contrast per task is
$\Delta_{WH}=\mathbb{E}_s[v_P-v_U]$ (contact-selection minus
direction-selection value); secondary estimands are the interaction
$I=\mathbb{E}_s[v_{PU}-v_P-v_U+v_0]$, oracle gain
$\mathbb{E}_s[v_{PU}-v_0]$, and two-way ANOVA variance shares of the table.
Because $v_P$, $v_U$, $v_{PU}$ are maxima, they are upward-biased under
measurement noise and $I$ inherits a positive bias; we therefore quantify the
noise floor directly with a zero-displacement control (identical primitive
with zero motion), whose $|Y|$ has median \zerodeltamed{} and 99th percentile
\zerodeltapninenine{} on this pilot, and we interpret $I$ only relative to
this floor.

\subsection{Validity and statistics}
A table is valid only if all 64 cells complete with successful grasp,
converged settle, finite outputs, and a re-placement error of the
canonicalized state within the $10^{-4}$\,m reset guard; any failure
invalidates the whole table (one retry, no cell deletion, no post-hoc state
replacement). Invalid tables
are reported separately from valid null results. Guards cover no-op drift
(re-settle and re-measure the same state), repeatability (identical tables
executed twice, including top-action equivalence with tie tolerance
$10^{-4}$), settle convergence rate, non-finite incidents, invalid-table rate,
and factor-dependent invalidity gaps.

Uncertainty uses a stratified (task $\times$ initial-shape) cluster bootstrap
over states with $10^4$ resamples; the three per-task primary contrasts form
one family with studentized max-$t$ simultaneous 95\% intervals. The
preregistered minimum absolute effect is $0.02$; a task is labeled
\emph{contact-dominant} only if $\Delta_{WH}\ge 0.02$ with simultaneous lower
bound $>0$, \emph{direction-dominant} symmetrically, else
\emph{balanced/inconclusive}. Cross-task contrasts are computed on the shared
strata \{u\_bend, s\_curve, random\_smooth\} with equal stratum weights, which
corrects a composition confound in the original preregistration (t1b/t1c
include a \emph{straight} stratum that t1a lacks).

\subsection{Deviations from preregistration}
This is a deadline-bounded pilot, and we report it as such:
(1) \nstatespertask{} development-split states per task instead of the
registered 96/64; (2) hidden-split confirmation and clean reproduction were
not executed, so labels below are development-split evidence only;
(3) cross-task contrasts use the shared-strata correction above;
(4) claim language is narrowed to contact-location versus fixed-magnitude
planar-direction choice; (5) the registered \emph{study-level} placement
guard ($\le 10^{-4}$\,m) is operationalized per table: states whose
canonicalized configuration cannot be re-placed within the guard yield
invalid tables that are quarantined rather than aborting the study. The
$8{\times}8$ grid, candidate definitions,
priority order, eligibility rule, guards, and estimands are unchanged from the
preregistration.

\paragraph{Interpretation caveats.}
A negative $\Delta_{WH}$ need not mean direction \emph{knowledge} is more
valuable in a constructive sense: a wrong planar direction actively pushes the
rope away from the goal ($Y<0$), while a wrong contact is often merely
unhelpful ($Y\approx 0$), so direction selection can dominate through
avoidance of harm. The sign structure of $v_0$ (grand mean
\vzerosummary) bounds how much of the effect is harm-avoidance. Second, the
asymmetric factor expressiveness (Section~1) means magnitude comparisons
between factors are parameterization-relative.

\section{Limitations}
This is a pilot: \nstatespertask{} states per task on the development split,
one simulator, one rope parameterization, no hidden-split confirmation, no
clean reproduction, and no grasp realism. Effects are one-step, greedy
quantities on canonicalized quasi-static states; they do not imply multi-step
policy design conclusions. The motion factor omits magnitude, vertical, and
rotational components; a $16{\times}16$ resolution probe and a
magnitude-augmented motion factor are the registered follow-ups. Sample sizes
imply wide intervals; per-task labels of \emph{balanced/inconclusive} should
be read as underpowered, not as evidence of equality.

\section{Conclusion}
We provide a preregistration-faithful, guard-validated pilot answering how
much one-step DLO progress is attributable to contact-location versus
fixed-magnitude planar-direction selection. The design --- complete factorial
tables, complete-table-or-invalid, effect-blind candidate selection, invalid
vs.\ null separation --- is reusable at full scale, and all per-cell raw data,
configs, and seeds are released for exact reproduction.
